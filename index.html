The Hubble Space Telescope:

The Hubble Space Telescope is a powerful telescope that orbits Earth and takes clear pictures of space. It is special because it is above Earth's atmosphere, so its images are much sharper than those taken by most telescopes on the ground. Since its launch in 1990, Hubble has helped scientists make many important discoveries about stars, planets, galaxies, and the universe.

Hubble orbits Earth at an altitude of about 540 kilometres and travels at around 28,000 kilometres per hour. It has observed hundreds of thousands of objects in space and has collected more than 1.5 million observations during its mission. Its discoveries have helped scientists estimate the age of the universe and study black holes, nebulae, and distant galaxies.

The telescope is named after the American astronomer Edwin Hubble, whose work showed that the universe is expanding. Hubble continues to provide valuable information to astronomers around the world and has inspired new space telescopes, such as the James Webb Space Telescope. Its amazing images have helped people better understand the beauty and size of the universe.
